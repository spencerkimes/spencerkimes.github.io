Hiking

New Zealand has absolutely incredible hiking throughout the counry. There are amazing national parks and the country has done a great job with conservation of wildlife areas and clean tourism. On my trip we visited 4 of the national parks that New Zealand has to offer and hiked 2 great walks, although there are many great hikes throughout the country.

Roy's Peak

Roy's Peak is a very steep, 5 hour trek to a peak overlooking the city of Wanaka. It offers incredible views the whole way up of what many would say is the most beautiful lake in the world.

Copland Track

I was lucky enough to experience "Tramping" in New Zealand where you hike up to a hut and spend the night there with other travelers. We hiked the Copland Track on the side of Mt. Cook for one of the most amazing experiences of my life. Hiking through all types of terrain and crossing huge passes via a tiny bridge was unlike anything I've ever done in my life.

Mt. Cook

We were actually lucky enough to hike up 2 different sides of Mt. Cook and I have to say this is definitely the way to go. In Mt. Cook National Park, you ascend up stairs very quickly and eventually get to a trail overlooking an incredible valley with snow capped peaks. This was my favorite hike of the trip and definitely worth the journey.

Kepler Track

The Kepler track is another great walk that is 4 days long if you complete the whole thing. Luckily there is a very popular 1 day trail that I was able to do. You start off hiking through forest and eventually come out above the tree line overlooking Lake Te Anau - another very beatiful lake in New Zealand.
